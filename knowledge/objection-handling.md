# Objection Handling

## Price
Objection:
"19,500 psf is higher than some nearby projects."

Suggested response:
"The price should be evaluated against the fully fitted specification, low-density planning, Hero brand trust, 60,000 sqft managed clubhouse, Panasonic smart-home ecosystem, and larger-format luxury positioning. This is not a shell-apartment comparison."

## Location
Objection:
"Is Sector 104 developed enough?"

Suggested response:
"Sector 104 sits on the Dwarka Expressway growth corridor. The project is positioned bang on the expressway, with access narratives around the airport tunnel, Global City, Yashobhoomi, Cyber City, and Delhi connectivity. Travel times are indicative and should be checked by route and time of day."

## Builder Trust
Objection:
"Is Hero experienced in ultra-luxury?"

Suggested response:
"Hero Realty is backed by Hero Enterprise, a group with a long brand legacy. The corporate presentation also highlights 6.02 million sqft delivered and 5700+ customers. For luxury credibility, the narrative can include Hero Enterprise's London real estate investment examples such as Holland Park Gate, while keeping claims source-grounded."

## Payment Plan
Objection:
"The payment plan is confusing."

Suggested response:
"The simple version is that the 30:70 plan staggers the first 30% over early milestones, and the next major 30% payment is described as tentatively after super-structure completion. It gives buyers more breathing room compared with heavier upfront structures."

## Resale
Objection:
"Will resale demand be strong?"

Suggested response:
"The resale argument should be based on corridor demand, branded developer trust, low-density supply, large-format apartment scarcity, and the project's expressway-facing luxury positioning. It is still market-linked, so buyers should compare current resale and rental data before booking."

## Rental Yield
Objection:
"Will the rental yield justify the price?"

Suggested response:
"For ultra-luxury homes, rental yield alone may not be the full story. The pitch should combine potential premium tenant demand, long-term appreciation, expressway connectivity, and lifestyle value. Investors should model yield separately from appreciation."
